## Reflection Answers

## 1. Why do we convert continuous values into categories?
- Raw numbers are hard to interpret quickly, especially for business teams.
- Categories like Gold/Silver make insights easier to understand and act on.


## 2. Difference between business segmentation and technical bucketing?
- Business segmentation follows fixed rules based on goals or strategy.
- Technical bucketing is data-driven and based on distribution (like quantiles).


## 3. When would fixed thresholds fail?
- When data distribution changes
- When customer spending patterns vary over time
- When thresholds are not representative of actual data


## 4. How does quantile-based segmentation differ?
- Quantiles divide data evenly based on actual distribution.
- Fixed rules stay constant and may not reflect real data patterns.


## 5. Which method is best in real-world?
- For business clarity, conditional logic works best.
- For dynamic data, quantile-based segmentation is more reliable.

Best choice depends on use case
